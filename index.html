Why walk in tubs in New York

Why New York homeowners are replacing slippery tubs with walk in tubs & roll in showers

New York homes range from compact city apartments and co-ops to historic brownstones, Hudson Valley farmhouses and lakeside cottages. Most of those bathrooms were designed decades ago, long before anyone thought about aging in place, bad knees or balance issues.

High tub walls, slick tile and tight spaces turn a quick shower into an obstacle course. Add in icy winters, bulky winter clothing and mobility challenges and it is easy to see why bathroom falls are such a serious risk for New Yorkers.

A modern walk in tub or roll in shower changes that story. Instead of swinging a leg over a high edge, you open a door, sit on a secure, chair-height seat and let warm water do the work. Grab bars, non-slip floors and easy-reach controls keep you supported from the moment you step in until you dry off.

For many New York families, the biggest benefit is peace of mind. Adult children can worry less when a parent lives alone upstate. Spouses can help with bathing in safer, more dignified ways. And seniors who once avoided bathing out of fear can enjoy a relaxing soak again.
