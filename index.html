Why walk in tubs in New York

Why New York homeowners are upgrading to walk in tubs & roll in showers

New York homes range from city apartments and co-ops to historic brownstones, Hudson Valley farmhouses and lakeside cottages. Most of those bathrooms were designed decades ago, long before anyone thought about aging in place, mobility challenges or joint pain.

High tub walls, slick tile and narrow layouts turn a quick rinse into an obstacle course. Add in icy sidewalks, heavy winter clothing and balance issues and it is easy to see why bathroom falls are such a serious risk for New Yorkers.

A modern walk in tub, NY walk in bath or roll in shower changes that story. Instead of swinging a leg over a high edge, you open a door, sit on a secure, chair-height seat and let warm water do the work. Grab bars, non-slip floors and easy-reach controls keep you supported from the moment you step in until you dry off.

For many families, the biggest benefit is peace of mind. Adult children can worry less when a parent lives alone upstate. Spouses can help with bathing in safer, more dignified ways. And seniors who once avoided bathing out of fear can finally enjoy a relaxing soak again.

Lower fall risk in tight spaces

Low step entry, textured floors and grab bars help reduce the most common causes of bathroom falls.

Warm water and jets help ease stiffness after walking city blocks, shoveling snow or standing on your feet all day.
